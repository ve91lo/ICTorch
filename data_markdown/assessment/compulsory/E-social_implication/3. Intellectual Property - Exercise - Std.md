# Questions and Answers

## 1. Explain the Difference between Patents and Copyrights. Provide Examples of what Each Protects and how They Are Used

- **Patents** protect inventions and processes. They grant the inventor exclusive rights to use, sell, and manufacture the invention for a certain period, typically 20 years.
- **Example**: A new type of engine design can be patented, preventing others from making or selling that engine without permission.

- **Copyrights** protect original works of authorship, such as literature, music, and art. They give the creator exclusive rights to reproduce, distribute, and display the work.
- **Example**: A novel written by an author is protected by copyright, meaning others cannot publish or sell the book without the author's consent.

## 1.2. What is the Purpose of Trademarks, and how Do They Differ from Copyrights? Provide Examples of Trademarked Items and Their Significance

- **Trademarks** are symbols, words, or phrases legally registered or established by use as representing a company or product. They protect brand identity and help consumers distinguish between different products.
- **Example**: The Nike "swoosh" logo is a trademark that signifies the brand and its products. It helps consumers identify Nike products and assures them of the quality associated with the brand.

### Significance of Trademarks
- Trademarks help maintain brand reputation and consumer trust.
- They prevent confusion in the marketplace by distinguishing products from different manufacturers.
